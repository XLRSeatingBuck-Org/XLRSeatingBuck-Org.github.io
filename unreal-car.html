Immersive Car Simulation in Unreal Engine

Driving through a detailed map of Corvallis using high-fidelity hardware in Unreal Engine.

Key Features and Workflow

Map Creation & Integration

The map of Corvallis is sourced from OpenStreetMap and then imported into CityEngine for rule-based modeling. This process involves designing the street network, buildings, and other urban structures. Afterward, the map is imported into Unreal Engine for final integration, where itâ€™s enhanced with additional environmental details and physics for the driving experience.

Realistic Car Physics

The car simulation is powered by realistic car physics in Unreal Engine, enabling dynamic interactions with the environment. Using input from hardware like manual transmission, steering wheels, and pedals, the car mimics real-world driving behavior, enhancing the immersion of the simulation.

Immersive VR Experience

The simulator integrates with VR headsets like Oculus Quest Pro and Varjo XR-4 to provide a fully immersive experience. Combined with haptic feedback devices like TactSuit X40, users can feel the simulation in a more tangible way, enhancing the realism of the driving experience.
